History Gym

Massive gyms (rocking chairs) began to appear in 1950 and were places where mainly men who were interested in bodybuilding or weightlifting trained. These guys mainly developed muscle performance and sought to increase muscle mass, at any cost.

More experienced athletes acted as trainers in such gyms, who gave recommendations on how many approaches should be done and how many repetitions. They relied solely on their sports experience and did not take into account the science, capabilities and needs of the Client.

In 1951, a certain Jack Lalane began hosting a fitness show on a popular national television channel. Jack promoted health-improving exercises and calorie control in his shows, changing the vector of fitness development towards health. The show was very popular and aired until 1984.

In the 1960s, women's studios, or “figure salons” as they were then called, began to gain popularity. They were already focused on losing weight and creating a slim figure. In these salons, instead of dumbbells and other fitness equipment, they mainly used simulators that did vibration massage. Such fitness on the couch

Also in the 1960s, President John F. Kennedy changed the name of the “Ministry” of Youth Affairs to the “Ministry” of Physical Education, now addressing this message not only to teenagers, but to the entire adult population. This provided significant support in the popularization of fitness and a healthy lifestyle in America.

In 1965, the first club of the global Gold Gym network was opened, which was imbued with the spirit of Bodybuilding and the embodiment of the movie “Pump Iron”. In the 70s and 80s, fitness clubs became increasingly accessible and popular. Men and women of all ages trained together indoors and outdoors, creating strong communities through healthy lifestyles and communication.

And the organization of mass “sports” events encouraged people to develop physical qualities, becoming stronger, faster, more dexterous. The fitness movement was actively gaining momentum.

But the concept of a fitness trainer has not yet been formed. These were the same enthusiasts who trained by experience, by feeling. Despite this, the growth of the fitness industry as a whole has led to people looking for help getting into the gym and being willing to pay for it. This is how to form a profession as a personal trainer.

In those years, it was not practice to ask the Client questions about previous experiences, illnesses, and goals. The training programs were formulaic, not based on the capabilities of each individual Client. The situation changed when Americans paid attention to how many people suffer from chronic diseases and how this harms their economy.

Awareness of these factors has led to the development of the personal trainer profession in a broader area than just a beautiful body. The emergence of Academies, which lay the foundation in the form of basic knowledge of the human structure + research, made trainers people who prolong life, help to work and spend free time more effectively. They also enable Clients to live enjoying developed functional abilities and a beautiful body.
